3

Comprehensive Client Intake Screening

For new client calls, the AI conducts thorough intake:

Universal Questions (All Practice Areas):

  1. Contact information (name, phone, email, preferred contact method)
  2. Case type and brief description
  3. How they found your firm (marketing tracking)
  4. Urgency level (statute of limitations, court dates, deadlines)
  5. Geographic location (jurisdiction check)
  6. Any prior legal representation on this matter
  7. Conflicts check (opposing party name/company)

Practice-Specific Questions:

Personal Injury: Date of incident, type of injury, medical treatment received, insurance info, police report filed, any settlement offers received

Family Law: Type of case (divorce, custody, support), marriage date, children (ages), current custody arrangement, domestic violence concerns, marital assets overview

Criminal Defense: Charges, arrest date, bail status, court date, previous criminal history, current legal representation, probation/parole status

Estate Planning: Estate size estimate, family structure, existing will/trust, specific concerns (special needs, business succession), timeline

Business Law: Business type/size, nature of legal issue, contract review/dispute/formation, timeline and urgency, budget range

All answers are documented in your practice management system automatically.

4

Lead Qualification & Case Evaluation

The AI applies your firm's qualification criteria:

  • Practice Area Match: "We specialize in personal injury. Your contract dispute would be better served by a business attorney. May I provide a referral?"
  • Jurisdiction Check: "We practice in California. Your Oregon case would require a local attorney. I can recommend someone."
  • Statute of Limitations: "When did the incident occur? [3 years ago] Unfortunately, the 2-year statute of limitations has expired. You may want to consult another attorney for a second opinion, but we likely cannot help."
  • Case Value Threshold: For high-volume firms, filters based on minimum damages/case complexity
  • Conflict Checks: Flags potential conflicts for attorney review before scheduling

Qualified leads: Immediately scheduled for consultation or transferred to available attorney
Unqualified leads: Politely declined with referrals when appropriate, saving hours of attorney time

5

Intelligent Consultation Scheduling

For qualified leads, the AI schedules consultations seamlessly:

  1. Checks attorney calendar in real-time (via Clio, MyCase, etc.)
  2. Offers available time slots: "I can schedule you with [Attorney Name] tomorrow at 2 PM or Thursday at 10 AM. Which works better?"
  3. Books appointment directly in calendar system
  4. Confirms consultation type (phone, video, in-person)
  5. Sends confirmation text/email with:
    • Date, time, attorney name
    • Meeting format and instructions
    • Documents to bring/prepare
    • Intake form link (if required)
    • Office location/parking (for in-person)
  6. Sends reminder texts 24 hours and 1 hour before consultation
  7. Easy reschedule/cancel option via text reply

Emergency Call Triage (24/7)

The AI recognizes emergency situations and escalates appropriately:

True Emergencies (Immediate Attorney Alert):

  • Recent arrest or booking
  • Court appearance within 48 hours

  • Child custody emergency
  • Search warrant execution

Action: AI collects critical details, immediately texts on-call attorney with summary, places caller on brief hold while calling attorney's cell, connects them live if attorney available, or takes detailed message with callback commitment if unavailable.

Urgent But Not Emergency:

  • Legal papers received (lawsuit, subpoena)
  • Court date scheduled (but not imminent)
  • Settlement offer received
  • Demand letter received

Action: Schedules priority consultation (next business day or within 48 hours), documents details thoroughly, sends secure message to attorney with case info.

Routine Calls (Standard Scheduling):

General consultations, estate planning, contract review, etc. - scheduled normally during business hours.

Law Firm Owner FAQs About AI Receptionists

Is this compliant with attorney-client privilege?

Yes, CallBird is designed specifically for legal compliance:

  • No Legal Advice: AI never provides legal opinions or case strategy - only information gathering and routing
  • Privileged Communications: When callers share sensitive information, AI recognizes it and transfers to attorney immediately
  • Encryption: All calls and data encrypted end-to-end using bank-level security
  • SOC 2 Compliance: Data stored in certified compliant systems meeting legal industry standards
  • No Unauthorized Recording: Calls only recorded if legally required and with proper consent
  • Clear Disclaimers: AI identifies itself and clarifies it cannot provide legal advice in every call

Many law firms use CallBird specifically because it's MORE compliant than human receptionists who might accidentally disclose privileged info or forget to get conflicts waivers.

What if the AI can't answer a caller's question?

The AI is trained extensively on law firm operations and your specific practice, but for questions beyond its scope:

  1. Legal Questions: Immediately clarifies it cannot provide legal advice and offers to schedule consultation with an attorney
  2. Complex Scenarios: Takes detailed information and transfers to attorney/paralegal with full context
  3. Technical Questions: (Specific court rules, obscure procedures) Routes to appropriate staff member
  4. Truly Unusual Situations: Captures the question, apologizes for not having an immediate answer, and commits to attorney callback within specific timeframe

You receive a dashboard notification for any call the AI couldn't fully handle so you can fill knowledge gaps and update the AI's training. Within 2-3 weeks, the AI learns your practice's unique nuances and handles 98%+ of calls independently.

How long does setup take for a law firm?

Most law firms are fully operational within 24-48 hours:

  • Day 1 - Hour 1: 45-minute onboarding call. We learn your practice areas, intake requirements, emergency protocols, existing systems (Clio, MyCase, etc.)
  • Day 1 - Hours 2-4: Our technical team integrates with your practice management software, sets up call routing, imports your calendar, configures intake forms
  • Day 1 - Hour 5: Testing phase. We simulate 15-20 different call scenarios (new client intake, emergency call, existing client, spam) while you listen and provide feedback
  • Day 1 Evening: Soft launch in "shadow mode" - AI handles calls but also forwards to your existing receptionist so you can compare and refine
  • Day 2: Full launch. AI handles all calls independently. We monitor closely for first week and make real-time adjustments

Solo practitioners often go live in 12 hours. Large firms (10+ attorneys, multiple practice areas) may take 3-5 days to customize all the call flows and integrations.

Can I still have a human receptionist too?

Absolutely. Many law firms use a hybrid model:

  • Option 1 - Time-Based: AI handles nights/weekends/holidays, human handles business hours
  • Option 2 - Overflow: Human answers when available; AI takes overflow and handles calls when receptionist is busy/lunch/out
  • Option 3 - Task-Based: AI handles all phone intake/scheduling; human focuses on in-person clients, mail, filing, complex admin
  • Option 4 - Full AI: Redeploy receptionist to paralegal work, client relations, or marketing while AI handles 100% of phones

The beauty of CallBird is flexibility. You can shift between these models instantly based on staffing needs, workload, or preference.

What happens if the AI makes a mistake?

First, mistakes are extremely rare after the initial training period (less than 1% of calls). But when they occur:

  • Real-Time Monitoring: You see every call transcript in your dashboard. If you spot an issue, you can intervene immediately
  • Human Escalation: If AI detects its own uncertainty mid-call, it transfers to human staff rather than guessing
  • Error Alerts: Any call where AI couldn't complete the intended action triggers a notification to you for follow-up
  • Continuous Learning: When you flag an error ("AI should have scheduled this as an emergency consultation, not routine"), we update the training immediately
  • Backup Contact: Every caller receives a text confirmation with your direct office number if they need to follow up

Compare this to human receptionists: they make mistakes too (wrong attorney assigned, incomplete intake, lost messages) but there's often no record or way to catch it until the client complains. AI mistakes are logged, traceable, and correctable.
