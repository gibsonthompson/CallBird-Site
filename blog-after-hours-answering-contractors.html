Setting Up Emergency Call Triage

The most critical feature of after-hours answering is proper emergency recognition. Here's how to set it up:

Emergency Keywords by Trade

Plumbing Emergency Keywords:

  • Burst pipe, broken pipe, pipe leak
  • Flooding, water everywhere, basement flooding
  • Sewer backup, sewage, toilet overflowing
  • No water, water shut off
  • Gas leak, smell gas
  • Water heater leaking

HVAC Emergency Keywords:

  • No heat (in winter), furnace not working
  • No AC, air conditioner not working (in summer)
  • Gas smell, carbon monoxide
  • Smoke, burning smell
  • Freezing, elderly/infant in home
  • Commercial facility down

Electrical Emergency Keywords:

  • Sparking, sparks
  • Smoke, burning smell
  • No power, power outage (if isolated to property)
  • Breaker won't stay on
  • Hot to touch, panel hot
  • Shock, electrical shock

Emergency Response Protocol

Tier 1 - Critical Emergency (immediate danger):

  • Gas leak, carbon monoxide, electrical fire, sewage backup
  • Action: Immediate text + call to on-call tech
  • Customer told: "I'm connecting you to our emergency technician right now"
  • Response time goal: Tech calls back within 2-5 minutes

Tier 2 - Urgent Emergency (major inconvenience):

  • No heat/AC, flooding, no water
  • Action: Text to on-call tech with 15-minute callback expectation
  • Customer told: "Our emergency tech will call you within 15 minutes"
  • Response time goal: Tech calls back within 15 minutes

Tier 3 - After-Hours Standard:

  • Problem needs attention but not immediate danger
  • Action: Book first available appointment (usually next morning)
  • Customer told: "I can get you scheduled for first thing tomorrow morning"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Not Testing Emergency Protocols

The Problem: You set up after-hours answering but never actually test an emergency scenario. When a real emergency happens at midnight, the notification doesn't work or goes to the wrong person.

The Solution:

  • Test emergency scenarios at least monthly
  • Call yourself using emergency keywords
  • Verify texts arrive and contain all necessary info
  • Confirm backup contacts work if primary doesn't respond

Mistake #2: Overly Complex Call Routing

The Problem: Creating too many tiers, routing rules, and exceptions. Customer calls at 10 PM on Saturday about a leak and gets routed through 4 different menus before reaching a human.

The Solution:

  • Keep it simple: Emergency vs Non-Emergency
  • Emergencies: Immediate notification
  • Non-emergencies: Book appointment
  • Maximum 2-3 questions before action

Mistake #3: Not Training Your Team

The Problem: You implement after-hours answering but don't tell your techs how it works. They get an emergency text at 2 AM and don't know what to do with it.

The Solution:

  • Walk through the entire system with all techs
  • Show them how to access call recordings
  • Practice emergency response procedures
  • Set clear expectations for response times

Mistake #4: Forgetting to Update Business Hours Everywhere

The Problem: You set up 24/7 answering but your Google Business Profile, website, and Facebook still say "Closed" at 6 PM. Customers don't call after hours because they think you're unavailable.

The Solution:

  • Update Google Business Profile to "24/7 Emergency Service Available"
  • Add "24/7 answering" to website header
  • Update all directory listings
  • Add it to voicemail message (for people who still leave voicemails)

Mistake #5: Not Tracking ROI

The Problem: You implement after-hours answering but never measure how many jobs came from it or what they're worth.

The Solution:

  • Tag all after-hours calls in your CRM
  • Track monthly: calls received, calls converted, revenue generated
  • Compare pre- and post-implementation metrics
  • Calculate actual ROI quarterly
