Key Features for Veterinary Clinics

Emergency Call Handling

The most critical feature for any vet clinic. Your AI receptionist should:

  • Recognize emergency keywords (poisoning, trauma, breathing difficulty, bleeding, seizures)
  • Ask appropriate triage questions ("Is the pet conscious?" "When did this happen?")
  • Either transfer immediately to on-call staff OR provide emergency clinic directions
  • Never tell a panicked pet owner to "call back during business hours"

üí° Emergency Keywords to Configure

Make sure your AI is trained to recognize: hit by car, not breathing, unconscious, seizure, poisoned, ate chocolate, ate medication, bleeding heavily, broken bone, can't walk, choking, collapsed, eye injury, bloated stomach, labor/giving birth

Appointment Type Recognition

Different visits need different time slots:

  • Wellness exam: 30 minutes
  • Sick visit: 30-45 minutes
  • Surgery consult: 45 minutes
  • Dental cleaning: Half-day block
  • New patient: 45 minutes (extra paperwork)

Your AI should ask the right questions to book the appropriate appointment type and duration.

Pet Information Collection

Before booking, the AI should gather:

  • Pet name and species (dog, cat, exotic)
  • Breed and approximate age/weight
  • Reason for visit / symptoms
  • Whether this is a new or existing patient
  • Owner's contact information

Frequently Asked Questions

Can the AI handle anxious pet owners?
Yes. Modern AI receptionists are trained to respond calmly and reassuringly. They can recognize distress and respond appropriately‚Äîeither by expediting emergency transfers or by providing calm reassurance for non-emergencies. However, for extremely emotional situations, services like Smith.ai with human backup may be preferable.
Does it work with my practice management software?
CallBird integrates with major practice management systems including eVetPractice, Cornerstone, and others via API. Smith.ai has more limited integrations. Always verify compatibility with your specific software before signing up.
What happens to after-hours emergency calls?
You configure the behavior: The AI can transfer to your on-call vet, provide directions to the nearest emergency clinic, take a message for urgent (but not emergency) calls, or any combination based on keywords detected.
Can it handle multiple doctors' schedules?
Yes. You can configure different availability for different veterinarians and let the AI book with the appropriate doctor based on availability, specialty, or caller preference.
What about exotic pets?
The AI can be trained to ask about species and route exotic pet calls appropriately‚Äîeither to a vet who sees exotics or to provide a referral to a specialist. You control the logic.
