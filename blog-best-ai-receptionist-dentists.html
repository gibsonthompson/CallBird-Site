HIPAA Compliance Requirements

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: HIPAA Compliance is NON-NEGOTIABLE

Any AI receptionist handling patient calls for your dental practice MUST be HIPAA compliant. Using a non-compliant solution exposes your practice to fines of $100-$50,000 per violation, plus potential lawsuits.

What HIPAA Requires for Phone Systems

Technical Safeguards:

  • End-to-end encryption for all calls and data
  • Secure data storage with access controls
  • Automatic timeout for inactive sessions
  • Regular security updates and patches

Administrative Safeguards:

  • Business Associate Agreement (BAA) required
  • Written policies and procedures
  • Workforce training on HIPAA
  • Incident response procedures

Physical Safeguards:

  • Secure server facilities
  • Device and media controls
  • Workstation security

Audit Controls:

  • Complete audit trails of who accessed what data when
  • Logging of all system activities
  • Regular compliance audits

How to Verify HIPAA Compliance

Questions to Ask Any AI Receptionist Provider:

  1. "Will you sign a Business Associate Agreement (BAA)?"
    • If NO ‚Üí Run away immediately. This is required by law.
    • If YES ‚Üí Request to see their standard BAA before committing
  2. "How is patient data encrypted?"
    • Look for: AES-256 encryption at rest and in transit
  3. "Where is patient data stored?"
    • Should be in SOC 2 Type II certified data centers
  4. "Who has access to patient data?"
    • Should be limited to essential personnel only
  5. "How long is data retained?"
    • Should align with your practice's retention policies

üö© Red Flags - Do Not Use if Provider:

  • ‚ùå Refuses to sign a BAA
  • ‚ùå Gives vague answers about security
  • ‚ùå Says "HIPAA compliant" but can't provide documentation

  • ‚ùå Has no incident response plan

Implementation Guide for Dental Practices

Step 1: Ensure HIPAA Compliance

Before signing up for ANY AI receptionist:

  1. Request and review the Business Associate Agreement (BAA)
  2. Verify encryption standards (AES-256 minimum)
  3. Confirm data storage locations (must be SOC 2 certified)
  4. Review security measures and audit logging
  5. Document everything for HIPAA compliance records

Step 2: Integrate with Practice Management Software

Dentrix Setup:

  • Connect API credentials
  • Map appointment types to Dentrix codes
  • Set provider-specific schedules
  • Configure buffer times
  • Test booking flow end-to-end

Eaglesoft Configuration:

  • Authenticate practice account
  • Configure procedure codes
  • Set up provider calendars
  • Map hygienist schedules

Step 3: Configure Appointment Types

Standard Appointments:

  • Adult cleaning: 60 minutes
  • Child cleaning: 45 minutes
  • New patient exam: 45 minutes
  • Crown preparation: 90 minutes
  • Filling: 60 minutes
  • Extraction: 45 minutes

Emergency Slots:

  • Same-day emergency: 30 minutes
  • Buffer between appointments
  • After-hours emergency protocol

Step 4: Set Up Emergency Protocols

Emergency Keywords AI Should Recognize:

  • "Severe pain" or "unbearable pain"
  • "Swelling" (especially facial swelling)
  • "Knocked out tooth" or "tooth trauma"
  • "Bleeding" that won't stop
  • "Broken tooth" with sharp edges
  • "Abscess"

Emergency Handling:

  • Immediate transfer to dentist if during office hours
  • After-hours: Collect details and escalate urgently
  • Emergency fee structure clearly communicated

Step 5: Configure No-Show Prevention

Automated Reminder Schedule:

  • 48 hours before: Confirmation call/text asking patient to confirm
  • 24 hours before: Reminder text with appointment details
  • 2 hours before: Final reminder text
  • If cancellation: Automatically offer slot to waitlist

Step 6: Train Your Team

Front Desk Training:

  • How AI handles calls (they can focus on in-office patients)
  • How to check appointments booked by AI
  • How to modify AI-booked appointments if needed
  • How to handle escalated calls

Doctor/Hygienist Training:

  • Review emergency call transfer process
  • Understand how patient intake works
  • Know how to access call recordings/transcripts
